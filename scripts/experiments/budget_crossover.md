# Capacity-vs-budget crossover — findings (Story H.f.4)

Generated by [`budget_crossover.py`](budget_crossover.py) over the 1,700-image
CIFAR-10 DR-1 subset (CPU, deterministic). Raw table:
[`budget_crossover_results.md`](budget_crossover_results.md); figure:
[`budget_crossover.png`](budget_crossover.png).

## Result

| model | 5ep | 10ep | 20ep | 40ep |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| `simple_cnn` | 0.275 | 0.349 | 0.274 | 0.403 |
| `resnet20`   | 0.292 | 0.293 | 0.351 | 0.277 |
| `mlp` (fixed) | — | 0.352 | — | — |
| `random` (fixed) | — | 0.095 | — | — |

## Finding: the crossover is *not* robust at this scale

The intended lesson — "the more-expressive CNN underperforms the flattened-pixel
MLP at a small budget and overtakes it once the budget is scaled" — does **not**
hold cleanly here. The curves are noisy and non-monotonic: `simple_cnn` ties the
MLP at 10 epochs (0.349), *drops* at 20 (0.274), then exceeds it at 40 (0.403);
`resnet20` **peaks at 20 epochs (0.351) and degrades at 40 (0.277)** — more budget
makes it *worse*.

## Why

The minimal teaching recipes deliberately omit an LR schedule and early stopping,
so on a 1,700-image subset longer training overfits / oscillates rather than
converging monotonically. The instability is the signal, not noise to hide: at
this data scale and regime, "more budget" is not reliably "better."

## Conclusion

A *robust* capacity-vs-budget demonstration needs (a) a proper training regime
(cosine / reduce-on-plateau LR schedule + early stopping) and/or (b) substantially
more data. You have to scale to see the effect cleanly — which is itself the
lesson (the legacy-model-vs-modern dynamic: capacity is latent until training and
data are scaled). The deferred larger-dataset study (a new DataRefinery instance +
GPU) is the path to a clean curve; this subset run is the honest baseline that
motivates it.
