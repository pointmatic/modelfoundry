# Full CIFAR-10 (50k) crossover — findings (Story H.f.7)

Generated by [`full_data_crossover.py`](full_data_crossover.py) over the full balanced
CIFAR-10 train set (`cifar10-base-full.yaml`, 28,330 train / 5,000 val / 16,670 test,
same 0.567/0.1/0.333 split proportions), CNNs on Apple-silicon **MPS** under the dynamic
regime (cosine + early stopping). Figure: [`full_data_crossover.png`](full_data_crossover.png).

## Result — the CNNs run away from the MLP; resnet20 edged by simple_cnn

| model | 1.7k | 10x (17k) | full (28k) | epochs run (full) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| random | 0.095 | 0.099 | 0.101 | — |
| sklearn MLP | 0.352 | 0.451 | 0.465 | 50 |
| `simple_cnn` | 0.403 | 0.544 | **0.669** | 40 |
| `resnet20` | ~0.35 | 0.594 | 0.646 | **17 (early-stopped)** |

Two readings:

**1. vs the MLP — the capacity-with-data story strengthens.** The sklearn MLP has
essentially plateaued (0.451 → 0.465, +0.01 from 10x → full), while both CNNs keep
climbing (`simple_cnn` +0.12, `resnet20` +0.05). At full data the CNNs sit ~0.18–0.20
*above* the MLP — the gap is wider than ever. More data favours the high-capacity
models; the flattened-pixel MLP can't use it.

**2. resnet20 vs simple_cnn — a reversal, but an explainable one.** At 10x `resnet20`
led (0.594 > 0.544); at full data `simple_cnn` edges it (0.669 > 0.646). The cause is
**early stopping × the LR schedule**, not capacity: `resnet20` **stopped at epoch 17**
(val_loss, patience 5) while its cosine schedule (`T_max=40`) was *still annealing* —
halted before the LR decay finished — whereas `simple_cnn` ran the full 40 epochs. The
higher-capacity model was cut off mid-schedule.

## Takeaway

The CNN-vs-MLP crossover is now emphatic and *widening* with data (H.f.6 → H.f.7). The
intra-CNN ordering, though, is sensitive to the **early-stopping × LR-schedule
interaction**: a `patience: 5` val_loss criterion can halt a cosine-annealed model
before its schedule completes, so a fair `simple_cnn` vs `resnet20` verdict at this
scale needs the regime tuned per model (longer patience, or `T_max` matched to the
actual run length) — a follow-up, not a capacity conclusion. The experiment surfacing
this subtlety is itself the value: scaling data resolved the *MLP* crossover cleanly,
but the *high-capacity-vs-high-capacity* comparison is regime-bound.
